About Turtle

Turtles are an order of reptiles known as Testudines, characterized by a shell developed mainly from their ribs. Modern turtles are divided into two major groups, the Pleurodira and Cryptodira, which differ in the way the head retracts.

Clutch size: Leatherback sea turtle: 110, Asian giant softshell turtle: 24 – 70

Scientific name: Testudines

Higher classification: Testudinata

Speed: Leatherback sea turtle: 35 km/h

Kingdom: Animalia

Order: Testudines; Batsch, 1788

About Cat

The cat is a domestic species of small carnivorous mammal. It is the only domesticated species in the family Felidae and is commonly referred to as the domestic cat or house cat to distinguish it from the wild members of the family

Daily sleep: 12 – 16 hours

Mass: 4 – 5 kg (Domesticated)

Speed: 48 km/h (Maximum)

Height: 23 – 25 cm

Lifespan: 12 – 18 years (Domesticated)

Scientific name: Felis catus

About Dog

The dog is a domesticated descendant of the wolf. Also called the domestic dog, it is derived from the extinct Pleistocene wolf, and the modern wolf is the dog's nearest living relative. The dog was the first species to be domesticated, by hunter-gatherers over 15,000 years ago, before the development of agriculture.

Daily sleep: 12 – 14 hours (Adult)

Speed:German Shepherd: 48 km/h, Greyhound: 72 km/h

Scientific name: Canis lupus familiaris

Lifespan: 10 – 13 years

Rank: Subspecies

Gestation period: 58 – 68 days
